Spelling Bee Assistant

  • Tweet

Spelling Bee Assistant is an add-on for Spelling Bee, the New York Times’ popular word puzzle. It’s packed with features that will boost your playing experience.

Follow your progress, see what lies ahead of you and if you want to, get a hint or two. Eventually, when you run out of ideas, reveal the missing words - no more waiting until the next day!

Spelling Bee Assistant is a great improvement for an already great game and it won’t spoil the fun. Promised!

Installation

  • Browser extension

    The browser extension loads the assistant automatically when you open the Spelling Bee web site.

    It is available in the Chrome Web Store, at Firefox Add-Ons and at Microsoft Edge Add-Ons.

  • Bookmarklet

    Alternatively you can use the bookmarklet. It will start the assistant with a mouse click.

    To install, grab the badge above and drag it to your browser’s bookmark menu.

Features

  • Toolbar

    • Grab the assistant by the title to drag it anywhere inside the game area
    • Toggle dark mode
    • Display the options panel
    • Minimize the assistant

  • Score so far

    Where have you got to so far and how far away is the top? How many points will you need to become Queen Bee? That’s the sort of question you get answered here.

    • ✓What you achieved
    • ?What you still can achieve
    • ∑The sum thereof

  • Spoilers

    Are there more words with four or five letters? Is there another pangram hidden in the hive? Or at least a good long term that moves you to the next level? The Spoiler panel won’t tell you the words, but it will tell you how many of each length still lie ahead of you.

  • Spill the beans

    This little face might know a thing or two that you don't, so watch it while you type!

    • 🙂 You’re on the right track
    • 🙁 You’re barking up the wrong tree
    • 😐 You didn’t type anything yet

  • Steps to success

    This panel gives you a nice overview of the different ranks with the added bonus of revealing the road to Queen Bee. The tier that you’re currently in is highlighted.

  • Surrender

    When you’re eventually out of words but are dying to know the solution, this button will end your curiosity! Use it wisely - there is no way back and you won’t be crowned Queen Bee either!

    The words you didn’t find will be linked to the respective terms in Google, maybe this will help you next time!

  • Set-up

    If some of these features aren’t right for you, simply disable them in this panel.

System requirements

Spelling Bee Assistant works with modern browsers and has been built with laptops or desktop computers in mind. The extension also works in the Kiwi browser under Android. The user experience on mobile devices might be limited due to the screen size. The installation on iOS hasn’t been tested.

Installing bookmarklets on a phone or a tablet is a bit complex but feasible. Google links to a number of similar tutorials. I tried some of them on Android and it worked just fine. Doesn’t it spoil the game?

There are other tools with a similar purpose, which - according to this article - the Spelling Bee community seems to enjoy. The challenge stays the same and so does the fun. But now you have a little helper at hand when you’re stuck in a corner.

What is a bookmarklet?

Bookmarklets are small pieces of code that can enhance the functionality of a website. They reside, just like regular bookmarks, in your browser’s bookmark menu. Unfortunately, there is no straightforward way to assign an icon to them.

Known issues

  • When synchronizing two devices after the game has been launched, Steps to success shows the wrong tier. This will be fixed in a future version. As a workaround you can reload the page for now. If you’re using the bookmarklet-version you can click on it to relaunch the assistant.
  • The emoji in Spill the beans doesn’t show, there is a rectangle instead. I have seen that only once on an ancient Linux. The underlying issue is that old fonts don’t always support emojis. A font update might help.
